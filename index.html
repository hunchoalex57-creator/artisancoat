Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What decorative concrete systems do you use and how do they work? +

We use several systems depending on your surface, location, and goal. For exterior surfaces like porches, stairs, and pool decks we use polymer-modified decorative concrete overlays — applied over existing concrete, stamped into stone or tile patterns in your choice of color. For interior floors and walls we use microcement — a seamless, ultra-thin coating with a modern finish. We also do concrete repairs, sealing, and staining. The right product depends on a proper diagnosis of your surface — that's always our first step.

Do you need to demolish my existing concrete? +

In most cases, no. Our resurfacing system is applied directly over your existing concrete — whether it's a porch, pool deck, patio, or stairs. We repair cracks and damaged areas first, then apply the new surface over top. This saves time, money, and eliminates the mess of demo work.

How long does a resurfacing project take? +

Most residential porch and stair jobs take 1–3 days. Pool decks may take 2–4 days depending on size. Microcement installations vary by scope. We'll give you a specific timeline during your free estimate — and we stick to it.

Will it hold up to Toronto winters? +

Absolutely. Our Jewelstone coatings are frost-resistant and formulated for freeze-thaw conditions. The coating flexes with temperature changes instead of cracking. Clients across the GTA have had our surfaces for years without winter damage.

How much does resurfacing cost compared to replacement? +

Resurfacing typically costs 40–60% less than full concrete replacement, which also involves demolition, disposal, forming, and pouring new concrete. With Artisan Coat, you get a better-looking result, faster — and no dumpster in your driveway.

Do you offer a warranty? +

Yes. All our resurfacing work comes with a 3-year warranty. We stand behind our work and are committed to making sure you're satisfied with the result.
